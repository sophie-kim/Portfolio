Iconography about the death of Alex Nieto



This project honors the life of Alex Nieto, whose death by gentrification makes him an influential San Franciscan.
The San Francisco Mission district native was wrongfully murdered by SFPD at Bernal Hill Park in 2014.
I chose to represent Alex Nieto's death in 3 icons:
  • 1. An unwrapped and unbitten burrito, since he did not get to eat it.
  • 2. A Forty-Niners sweatshirt with bullet holes through it, since he was racially profiled as having been gang affiliate because he was wearing a red hoodie,
    and his hand in his pocket to represent the fact that SFPD started shooting before giving him time to put his hands up
  • 3. A gun branded with SFPD's logo with bullet shells belonging to the bullet holes that ripped through his hoodie.
